Section 2

Enterprise targets & what changes

Each enterprise below is sized, priced, and sequenced against its specific $200K contribution. Red text marks the specific changes from the base plan. These are not aspirational — each is grounded in the pricing and yield data already in your farm documents.

🫒
Olive grove — table olives
Target: $80,000 net / yr by Year 4
300 trees at full recovery. The change: sell table olives direct to retail and restaurants at $22–35/kg, not EVOO at $18/L wholesale. Manzanillo and Kalamata are premier table varieties. At 15,000kg recovered fruit, 60% premium-grade whole table olive = 9,000kg × $25/kg average = $225,000 gross. After curing costs, packaging, and operating: ~$80K net. Organic certification from 2030 adds a further 20–30% margin.
Primary enterprise Existing grove
🪲
BSFL — full commercial scale
Target: $48,000 net / yr by Year 3
Three changes from the base plan: (1) Reprice gate fees to $60–80/tonne (not $20 — legitimate market rate). (2) Scale to Phase 2 (450kg waste/week, 10–16 IBCs). (3) Add dried larvae channel at $22/kg for aquaculture buyers beyond the pet trade. Phase 2 gross: $2,145–4,050/month. At midpoint and $60/tonne gate fees: ~$55K gross, ~$48K net.
Primary enterprise Scale existing
🐝
Honey + commercial pollination
Target: $40,000 net / yr by Year 3
Two revenue streams, not one. Honey at 50 hives: 50 × 30kg × $20/kg average (Langstroth + native blend) = $30,000 gross. Commercial pollination: 50 hives × 3 seasonal placements × $100/hive = $15,000/season. Combined gross ~$45K, net ~$40K. The Lockyer Valley is avocado and macadamia country — you are already in the right location. The commercial pollination guide in your project files covers exactly this pathway.

Aquaponics — scaled to 15,000L
Target: $30,000 net / yr by Year 3
The base plan has a 5,000L system producing ~$16K gross. Scale to three 5,000L tanks (15,000L total) housed in the shed's dedicated aquaponics bay. At 300 fish per tank × 500g plate weight × $22/kg = $9,900/cycle per tank. Three tanks at 1.5 cycles/year = $44,550 gross, ~$30K net after feed, fingerlings, and power. The BSFL operation already provides supplemental feed — this integration is already planned, just not sized for 3 tanks.

Section 7

Risks specific to the $200K plan

These are the risks that don't exist in the base plan — the incremental risks of scaling up. The base plan risks (DA delay, bore capacity, etc.) are already covered in your ISO 31000 risk framework.

Olive variety mismatch — table olive grades lower than projected
The $80K olive target assumes 60% premium grade whole table olive. If Arbequina rows dominate (oil variety, small fruit, poor table grade) or if Kalamata has chilling hour issues, grade yield falls. Mitigation: Agronomist assessment May 2026 specifically requests grade yield estimate. If grade split is worse than 40% premium, revise olive target to $45–55K and compensate via BSFL or aquaponics scale-up.
High
QRIDA loan insufficient for larger shed + 3 tanks
The base QRIDA plan is $75K. The $200K plan needs ~$93K in construction-phase capex. The gap (~$18K) needs to be funded from Year 1 BSFL + hemp + egg revenue, a second QRIDA loan tranche, or a commercial top-up. Mitigation: Present the full $200K business case to QRIDA from the start — the stronger revenue projections support a larger loan. The equity position ($496K) easily supports $93K.
Medium
BSFL waste suppliers resistant to $60/tonne gate fees
Cafés and small food businesses may balk if they've heard about $20/tonne. Mitigation: Don't start the conversation at $20 — it's nearly impossible to renegotiate upward. Approach new suppliers at $60/tonne as the opening price. For existing contacts, frame as a commercial service from day one. Compare against commercial waste disposal costs ($100–180/tonne) — you are still demonstrably cheaper.
Medium
Aquaponics 3-tank system overwhelms management capacity in Year 1
Three 5,000L tanks is a meaningful management step up from one. Water quality, stocking, feeding, and harvest logistics triple. Mitigation: Stock Tank 1 at the same time as the base plan (March 2027). Stock Tank 2 at Month 6 once parameters are stable. Stock Tank 3 at Month 12. The CHOP2 design is forgiving — the risk is lower than it sounds.
Low-Med
Pollination contracts not secured before hive investment
Scaling to 50 hives only makes economic sense if pollination revenue is secured alongside honey. Mitigation: Approach macadamia and avocado growers in Q2 2026 (Jul–Sep) for the 2027 season — they book 6–9 months ahead. One signed contract at $80/hive × 20 hives covers the entire splitting programme cost.
