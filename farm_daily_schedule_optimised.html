Tasks that remain 9–5 unavoidable and why they can't move
Hive inspections (Jun–Oct, 10am–2pm): bees need ambient warmth above 18°C and calm conditions. Winter mornings are too cold and evenings too dark and cold. No workaround.
Zucchini flowers (Oct–Feb, 6–9am): flowers physically open at sunrise and close by 10am. Actually falls outside 9–5 — just flagged because it's time-critical.
Hemp DAF compliance (Nov–Jan): inspectors work business hours. Can't control this — schedule other tasks around it when an inspection is booked.
Honey extraction (Feb, 2 days/yr): warm ambient temperature assists flow, and extraction is a multi-hour process. Realistically a 7am–2pm job on those days.
Wasabi delivery (Mar–May): rhizome harvested in morning, restaurant delivered same afternoon. The drive to Toowoomba falls in business hours but the work either side of it does not.
Olive leaf drying (Aug): trays need turning every few hours outdoors during daylight. Can start at sunrise and finish before 5pm — partially avoidable with good morning start.
